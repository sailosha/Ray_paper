Two views of the same sound. Top: a density map of the harmonograph trace — brighter regions are where the curve passes more often, revealing where the standing-wave pattern concentrates its energy. Bottom: the raw frequency spectrum, with each harmonic plotted at its measured Hz and the bar height scaled by acoustic amplitude (amp = 10dB/20), so the tallest bar is genuinely the loudest harmonic in the sound.

Reading the heatmap. The harmonograph traces a single closed (or near-closed) Lissajous figure determined by the harmonics. Where the curve loops back over itself or slows down, pixels are visited many times and glow yellow-white; sparsely traced regions stay dark purple. The same color scale is used for the spectrum bars, so the loudest harmonic is also the brightest bar — the sketch above and the chart below are two complementary readings of the same sound.
