Music & Math Simulations

Harmonograph visualizations of traditional Chinese percussion instruments.

Each visualization reconstructs the harmonic spectrum of a real instrument — the frequencies and decibel readings of its first overtones — and renders them as a four-axis Lissajous-style harmonograph. The result is a moving portrait of the sound itself: the louder a harmonic, the more it shapes the figure; the higher its frequency, the denser its inner weave.
